KEY FINDINGS What the data reveals about bike buyers
💰
Income Is a Strong Signal
Buyers earn $3,088 more on average. Male buyers ($60,124) vs non-buyers ($56,208) show the clearest gap.
🛣️
Short Commutes Drive Sales
0–1 mile commuters generated 200 purchases — the single biggest commute segment. Intent drops sharply beyond 5 miles.
🎂
Middle Age Sweet Spot
Ages 31–54 account for 383 of 481 purchases. Only bracket where buyers outnumber non-buyers (383 vs 318).
🌏
Pacific Converts Best
Smallest region (192 customers) yet 58.9% conversion — 14 points above N. America's 43.3%.
💼
Professionals Lead
150 of 276 professionals bought — 54.3% rate. Graduates (54%) and Bachelors (55.2%) also above average.
🚗
Fewer Cars = More Bikes
0-car owners: 61.1% conversion (151/247). As cars increase, purchase intent drops sharply — to 35.6% for 2-car owners.
